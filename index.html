Amazonic Rain Forest Mining Activity "Region of Peru"

Introduction

The Amazon rainforest plays a vital role not just to South America but to the entire world!. The Amazonia represents over half of the planet's remaining rainforest and comprises one of the largest and most biodiverse ecosystems in the world. If we want to have a future as a species and leave a better world to our children we need to take hands into action and present the data so that we can make a difference. With this purpose, we are going to show the evolution of the mining activity in the Peruvian Amazon forest. In the last year, gold mining has destroyed almost 10000 hectares of rainforest in the Peruvian Amazon region, almost the size of Paris of rainforest was destroyed. This activity is not only destroying the rainforest, which is considered as the "lung of the world" because of the key role it represents fighting the climate change, but is also contaminating the soil and rivers with toxic compounds that are used to extract and processed the gold This compounds are not only killing both terrestrial and aquatic life, but also are dragged by the rivers to nearest towns where high levels of mercury have been detected. There are two different types of mining types in the Amazonia: “Heavy Machinery“ that uses big trucks and heavy machines to extract the gold from the soil, and “Suction Pumps” which is composed of washers and more traditional methods to extract the gold. We will analyze each one of this methods and present them as a timeline and an interactive visualization to show the effects that this activity is causing.

Heavy Machinery and Suction Pumps.
During the period: 1985– 2017

1985

The first record of gold mining at this region is in 1985, as you can see in the map it started with 73.9 ha and distributed between the sectors : Huepetuhe, Delta and Small Mines. At this time the sector with the highest percentage of mining activity was : Huepetuhe/Heavy Machinery with 35.73 ha. The relation between the two different mining methods was 55% for Heavy Machinery and 45% for Suction Pumps.

1993

In the year 1993, we can observe an alarming increase in mining activity, with a total increase of 1036%. The area of the mining activity was 839.5 ha distributed between the sectors: Huepetuhe, Delta and small mines. As you can observe the sector with the highest percentage remain as Huepetuhe with 695.6 ha of Heavy Machinery mining, this method had the 83% of the total mining area at that time. This was the preferred method to extract gold at this time, which can be confirmed comparing the increase in percentage compared to the previous year with an incredible 1618%.

2001

The things remain almost the same in 2001 in terms of total area used for mining with an increase of 10% with a total of 919.6 ha.
However if we go deeper into the data we can see that we had an abrupt change between the preferred method to mine the gold Suction Pumps had a total of 77% compared to just 17% in the previous period. The total area of Suction Pumps was 708 ha. and an increase of 392%, mostly distributed on the sector of small mines. And the Heavy Machinery decreased by 70 % with a total of only 211.1 ha.

2009

In 2009 the increase of mining activity compared to 1985 was alarming, an increase of 151% over the area used for mining, a total of 2304.6 ha of the Peruvian Amazon rainforest was destroyed and contaminated because of the gold mining. The sectors where the mining was done: Huepetuhe, Delta, Pampa, and small mines. The sector with the highest percentage was : Pampa with 962.47 ha of suction pumps mining. The relation between the two different mining methods was 20%-80% for heavy machinery and suction pumps. We can see that the suction pump remains as the main method for mining the gold. We have an increase of 161% for suction pumps with a total of 1846.1 ha and an increase of 117% for heavy machinery and a total of 458.4 ha

2017

In 2017 things became critical, the gold mining activity had an increase of 328% compared to 2009, with an area of 9859.8 ha, almost the size of Paris of Peruvian Amazon rainforest was destroyed and contaminated. At this moment the activity was distributed over the sectors: Huepetuhe, Delta, Pampa, and small mines. The sector with the highest percentage of mining is Small Mines with 4486.9 ha of Suction Pumps. The relation between the two different mining methods was 34%-66% for heavy machinery and suction pumps. We can see that the suction pump is the main method for mining gold, with an increase of 252% and a total of 6497.5 ha compared to an increase of 633% for heavy machinery and a total of 3362.3 ha.
